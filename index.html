Chitwan National Park

Chitwan National Park, Nepal’s first national park, was established in 1973 and is a UNESCO World Heritage Site. Covering 952.63 sq. km in the subtropical lowlands of southern Nepal, it is famous for its diverse wildlife, including the one-horned rhinoceros, Bengal tigers, elephants, and gharial crocodiles. The park offers jungle safaris, canoeing, and birdwatching, making it a popular destination for nature lovers and adventure seekers.
